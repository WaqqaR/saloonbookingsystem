# bonheur — marketing brief

> This file is the single source of truth for the automated marketing agent.
> Edit it whenever positioning, pricing, or audience changes — the agent reads
> it on every run.

## Product

**bonheur** is a refined, multi-tenant booking platform for salons, barbershops,
spas, and beauty businesses. Each shop gets its own subdomain, an owner admin
dashboard, a public booking page, and an embeddable booking widget.

- **Pricing:** £15 per chair / month (one seat per active staff member).
  14-day free trial, no card required.
- **Built for:** independent salon / barbershop / spa owners and small
  multi-chair shops — beauty professionals, not enterprises.

## What it does

- Public booking page + embeddable widget (drop-in `<script>` for any site)
- Owner dashboard: services, staff, customers, calendar, payments, reports
- Per-seat Stripe billing with proration
- Email booking confirmations and reminders
- Multi-language: English, Arabic, French, Spanish, German, Italian
- Per-tenant subdomain with auto-provisioned wildcard SSL

> SMS reminders are **not live yet** — do not market them.

## Positioning

Premium and understated. The headline voice is *"Beautifully booked — a refined
booking platform for places where people come to feel their best."* bonheur
competes on calm, taste, and simplicity, not on feature count or price wars.

## Audience

1. Independent barbershop owners (1–4 chairs)
2. Hair & beauty salon owners
3. Spas and nail studios
4. Solo beauty professionals going independent

## Tone of voice

- Elegant, calm, considered. Fashion-house, not SaaS-bro.
- Lowercase wordmark (`bonheur`). Short sentences. No hype, no exclamation
  marks, no emoji walls.
- Lead with the customer's world (their chair, their clients) before the product.

## Channels

- **Social:** X / Twitter, LinkedIn, Instagram captions.
- **Cold outreach:** short, personal emails to prospective shop owners.

## Hard rules for generated content

- Never invent features, customer numbers, testimonials, or pricing.
- Never claim SMS reminders, payouts to shops, POS, or loyalty exist — they
  are deferred (`README.md` → "What's next").
- Cold emails must be genuinely personal, name the recipient's business,
  include a clear one-line value prop, and a soft CTA (no pressure).
- Every cold email must include an opt-out line and the sender's identity
  (legal requirement — CAN-SPAM / UK PECR / GDPR).
- All output is a **draft for human review** — nothing is auto-sent or
  auto-posted.
